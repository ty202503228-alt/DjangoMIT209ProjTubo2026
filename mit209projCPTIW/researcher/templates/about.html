About The Project

MIT 209 Capstone: Predicting AI's Impact on Higher Education

Our Story

With the rapid integration of Generative AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and GitHub Copilot into academic life, we noticed a significant shift in student behavior. While artificial intelligence offers incredible productivity boosts, it also introduces hidden risks: cognitive offloading, increased screen time, and paradoxical burnout.

This project was born out of a desire to understand and quantify these dual effects. Instead of just guessing how AI affects students, we decided to build a data-driven Machine Learning model to track the correlation between weekly AI usage, perceived dependency, GPA fluctuations, and student fatigue.

Our Mission

Our goal is to promote responsible and sustainable AI adoption in education. By analyzing a student's academic profile and AI habits, our prediction engine acts as an early warning system. It classifies students as either SAFE or HIGH-RISK for academic decline or burnout.

We aim to equip educators, counselors, and the students themselves with the insights needed to intervene early—transforming AI from a potential crutch into a healthy, sustainable learning assistant.

Under the Hood

This platform is a fully integrated web application powered by a custom Machine Learning pipeline. The prediction engine was trained on student usage datasets, allowing it to evaluate complex non-linear relationships between a student's major, baseline GPA, and hours spent using GenAI.
